Abstract

We propose LLRM, a generalizable 3D Gaussian reconstruction model that is capable of reconstructing a large scene from 32 source images at 960x540 resolution within only 2 seconds on a single A100 80G GPU. Our architecture features a mixture of the novel Mamba2 blocks and the transformer blocks, facilitated by simple token merging and Gaussian pruning steps, showcasing a perfect balance between quality and efficiency; we patchify and tokenize the source images, feed the concatenated tokens into the sequence of Mamba2 / transformer blocks, and unpatchify and predict per-pixel Gaussian parameters at the end. Unlike past generalizable 3D Gaussian models, which are only able to take 1~4 input images and thus only able to reconstruct one corner of the scene, LLRM reconstructs the entire scene in one shot through a feed-forward step. We show comparable results to 3D-GS, whose results take >10 minutes to optimize, on large scene datasets including DL3DV-140 and Tanks and Temples.
